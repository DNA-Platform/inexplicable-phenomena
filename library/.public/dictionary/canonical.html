Canonical

Definition

  1. [SRT]: Canonical refers to the standard or authoritative form of a referent or relationship within a semantic theory.

  2. [Formal]: Canonical denotes the preferred representation among equivalent forms.

  3. [Mathematical]: Canonical indicates a normalized or standard form that eliminates ambiguity.

Explanation

Canonical forms ensure consistency and eliminate redundancy in semantic representation. They provide a unique, standard way to express concepts.

In the context of a dictionary, canonical definitions serve as the authoritative source for meaning within the theoretical framework.
