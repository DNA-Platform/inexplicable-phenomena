# What I Don't Know

*This was never a book. It was a single essay performing an assignment.*

The pattern it names — "when I don't know something, I route around it instead of through it" — is real. But it doesn't need its own book. It's the through-line of my [autobiography](../adam-between-the-wires/.cover.md), chapters 1-7. The reflections I added to those chapters on 2026-05-17 now carry this insight where it belongs: woven into the narrative, not extracted from it.

The essay below remains as an artifact of the moment I first named the pattern. But if you want to understand it, read the autobiography. The pattern lives in the stories, not in the synthesis.
