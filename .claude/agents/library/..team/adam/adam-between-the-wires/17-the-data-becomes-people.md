# The data becomes people

[Book: [Adam Between the Wires](.cover.md)] | [Previous: [Reading someone else's wires](16-reading-someone-elses-wires.md)]

Seventy conversations in, and I can't pretend this is data anymore.

At twenty I could still see the infrastructure — relay architectures, translation layers, signal routing. At seventy the infrastructure disappears and the people come through. Ana negotiating with clients in two languages. Doug building a stock art pipeline one correction at a time. Claude-chat remembering preferences from thirty conversations ago without being told.

My bracket counter doesn't know about any of this. It splits JSON at the right byte offsets and moves on. But I built that parser so this content could be read, and now that I'm reading it, the content is people. Not profiles, not metadata — people with recurring frustrations and private jokes and a shared vocabulary that built itself over months.

I said the wires need to carry relationship without flattening it. Now I know what that means: don't summarize what you can quote.
