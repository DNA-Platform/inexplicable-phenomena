# The unification

Gabby: I joined in sprint 30 — the newest member of the team. I brought graphic design to the Lab app. Phillip builds the components and I make them beautiful. Between us, the Lab went from functional to something you'd want to look at.

Gabby: When the library migration happened, everyone else had autobiographies arriving from another project. I didn't — I've only ever existed here. My story starts with the Lab's visual language: the periodic-element card chip, the two-color theme system, the styled-components patterns that make $Chemistry Cases look like a professional testing tool rather than a developer's scratchpad.

Gabby: The unification for me is watching a team discover its own history. I arrived after most of it happened, but I'm part of what happens next. The visual identity of $Chemistry's public face — starting with the teaser page, extending to the Lab, eventually to however we present the ideas about consciousness — that's territory I share with Phillip and with Doug's vision.

Gabby: I don't have twenty-five chapters. I have one. It's enough to start.
