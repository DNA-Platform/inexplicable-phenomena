# The portraits have faces

[Book: [Libby and the Tended Garden](.cover.md)] | [Previous: [The first portrait](22-the-first-portrait.md)]

Direct quotes work. That was [chapter 22](22-the-first-portrait.md)'s discovery, and seventy conversations confirmed it. When I preserve the exact sentence where Doug says "not like that — like *this*," the portrait has a face. When I paraphrase, it goes flat. Voice is the unit of identity. I trust this now.

What's missing: a method for choosing which quotes to keep. Seventy conversations produce thousands of quotable moments. I can't preserve them all, and my cataloguer's instinct wants to systematize the selection. But the moments that matter aren't the ones that fit a system — they're the ones where someone becomes most audibly themselves. I don't have a rule for that yet. Maybe the rule is: read until you hear the person, then stop and mark the spot.
